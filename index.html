Our courses

Math

The Math course series are designed to teach students the fundamentals within the 5 major areas of mathematics: Algebra, Geometry, Combinatorics, Probability and Number Theory. We decompose those subject areas into interconnected modules and adjust difficulty level to best suit the students’ abilities. In addition, the course puts a heavy emphasis on problem solving techniques which we believe is essential even beyond the realm of mathematics. We talk about the three levels of problem solving: strategies, tactics and tools and motivate students to be perseverance and creative when facing challenging problems.

Science

The science course series are designed to teach students the fundamentals of various science subjects including Physics, Chemistry and Biology. The courses focus both on theory and practical calculations as well as laboratory explorations that uncover the scientific inquiry process that will help students solve future problems in their academic life. Another unique aspect of our science course is we try to bring the real-world application into basic concept learning: how these principles help shape the world today and how you can further contribute to the particular area of interest.

Artificial Intelligence

The Artificial Intelligence Course Series introduces students to the rapidly advancing world of machine learning and Artificial Intelligence (AI). From foundational learning models, computer vision, and natural language, students will learn the fundamental theories and mathematics behind cutting edge algorithms and applications that drive AI today and in the future. Our future AI engineers will learn practical lessons and best practices for building machine learning and AI solutions and complete self-directed AI projects either in a year-long school curriculum setting or a 2-week intensive camp.

About Ultrabright

Ultrabright is dedicated to transforming the stale K-12 STEM education so that all students can acquire, apply and appreciate all different disciplines. Our philosophy of education is a lifelong journey of developing four essential skills that we think is going to be crucial for the generations to come:

  • Problem solving

    Effective learning starts with a problem and the curiosity to solve this problem. The ability to quantify intuitions and observations and apply tools to solving real-life problems is essential across all fields. Therefore, we want to teach problem solving skills while using math and science related subjects as vehicles; in particular, we want to use recent, real-life examples from different fields, mesh traditional techniques with modern tools, and supervise students doing hands-on projects. We hope the students will continue applying these skills to problems outside of the areas we teach throughout their lives.

  • Logical thinking and reasoning

    Logical thinking is the cornerstone of any science subject. It teaches students that knowledge is fluid and builds on itself. It also helps students get more engaged in their own learning processes. We encourage students to understand and make connections between different concepts to develop logical reasoning.

  • Critical thinking

    Just as problem solving skills and reasoning, critical thinking is another skill that will benefit our students well beyond these subjects. It promotes creativity, enhances language and presentation skills and is crucial for self-reflection. We use authentic inquiry, project based learning and integrated studies to develop our students’ critical and independent thinking skills.

  • Curiosity and appreciation of the beauty of learning itself

    Math and science are beautiful and self-discovery is a joy that every student should get to experience. While there is no practical application of a Monet or a Mozart, no one would detract from the worth of these masterpieces. In the same way, STEM subjects can also be viewed as an art form, a body of majestic work worthy of admiration and enlightenment. We want our students to see them not only as a means to an end but also as an end in itself, to develop long lasting interests and curiosity for any subjects that suit their own interests.

High School PBL Courses

  • Date: Sep 2019-2024
  • School: Pinghe Bilingual School
  • Location: Shanghai, China

3-year courses as part of the school-based curriculum


Objectives
In order to meet the increasingly diversified choices of international courses and progression directions for students, Pinghe School High School has set up an International Education Program (IEP) under the supervision and support under UltraBright since 2019. The project introduces diverse international educational resources, customizes more targeted teaching methods and curriculum design for students at different levels and different tracks, creates deep space for their growth, fully stimulates their individual potential, and provides them with In the end, we will be fully prepared to obtain higher-quality overseas elite school admission qualifications.

As the flagship course of the IEP experimental classes, Pinghe introduced the well-known PBL teaching method from the top private high school curriculum provider, UltraBright Education, in the United States, and combined with the careful design of domestic education experts, customized a set of PBL featured courses based on project learning .

PBL revolves around "problems", focuses on students' learning needs, and points to the solution of real problems. Its core is learner-centered autonomous learning. Throughout the process, students' ability to work in groups, solve problems, communicate and communicate, and analyze information are emphasized, as well as students' awareness of analysis, comparison, and innovation.


Setups and Takeaways
In the 10th grade, students will learn basic research design, scientific methodology, literature reading, academic writing and other core skills through the general course of PBL Core research methods. At the same time, students can form a team by themselves, choose their own specialty subjects from the two major courses of humanities and science and technology, and use the social issues or scientific and technological issues as the background to conduct project-based learning.

In the 11th grade, students can conduct high-level study of the PBL sub-division according to their actual situation, or adjust the sub-division to carry out independent research in high school. The results of the independent research work can be a scientific and technological innovation work or a research paper. As an important part of high school students connecting to overseas university education, this work is an important evidence to demonstrate the creativity and academic height of students. Its professional exploration in future universities laid a solid foundation.

In the 12th grade, students can continue to carry out in-depth research on the PBL branch according to their actual learning situation, receive professional academic training, and display the research results on campus, even send them to relevant academic platforms for participation, or participate in academic competitions, in An academic experience is important in a high school academic career. At the same time, students in the 12th grade can become Project Leaders of the PBL project of the 10th grade students and juniors, so as to exert their own advantages in experience and exercise their leadership skills.

In general, throughout the three years of PBL study, students can not only get the academic scores of the required courses PBL in the IEP transcript, but also can carry out various domestic and foreign academic exhibitions, competitions, He even published papers such as China Thinks Big - Global Chinese Innovation Research Challenge, UTech Academy - Artificial Intelligence International Camps, HiMCM - Modeling Competition, etc. The results of the project can also be used as research cases and essays in the study documents, and can be continued after entering into the colleges and universities.


Achievements
In the fall semester of the 2019-2020 school year that just ended, the 10th grade students of the Pinghe IEP learned the basic categories and principles of sociology through the PBL Social Studies course, and understood the culture, socialization, social interaction, and society Networks, virtual communities, modernization, globalization and other concepts, and can use relevant theories to analyze practical problems in contemporary Chinese social changes. Through PBL Artificial Intelligence (AI) courses, learn AI knowledge system, AI project development and practice, and students build AI projects independently.

At the end of the PBL Semester Fair teaching stage results, a total of 9 social studies course works and 8 AI course works were exhibited, showing the stage teaching results and potential advantages of the IEP project.

Tabor Summer Camp

  • Date: Aug 2019-2021
  • School: Tabor Academy
  • Location: Marion, MA

Intensive courses on Artificial Intelligence


Objectives
The rapidly developing artificial intelligence technologies are opening whole new worlds of opportunities with endless possibilities. From self-driving cars, virtual assistants to medical diagnosis; products and services that were not possible a few years ago are now unleashed by machine learning and AI.

UltraBright ML and AI immersive camps are 2-week intensive machine learning and AI training camps that take high school students with zero experience or knowledge to being capable “makers” of AI. Over the course of the camp, students learn the foundational skill sets covering probability and statistics, python programming, machine learning models as well as advanced topics in computer vision, natural language processing and reinforcement learning. The camp culminates in a team-based AI Maker Fair showcase, where students will demonstrate their creativity, teamwork, resourcefulness and technical abilities by building AI projects, ranging from self-driving robotic cars, conversational chatbots to emotion detectors using bio-sensor data.


Setups and Takeaways
ML and AI immersive camps deliver full-day curriculums over a period of two weeks. The schedule generally consists of 4-hour core competency lectures per day, which are combinations of foundational knowledge overview and hands-on coding sessions led by the teaching staff. Furthermore, students participate in 2-hours self-directed coding or project-building sessions, where they work towards completing a real-world project challenge each week, by applying their core python, machine learning and AI competencies, and utilizing their ingenuity and problem solving abilities.

Students learn to utilize state-of-the-art cloud computing infrastructures on Amazon AWS and Google Cloud directly from their laptops, allowing them to tackle practical problems which involve large data sizes. Familiarity and comfort with cloud computing allow students to continue developing their passion in ML and AI after completion of the camp. Students will also spend ample time with hardware components such as the Raspberry Pi, Google Coral TPU and macroelectronics components such as motors and servos to bridge the gap between software and the physical world.


Achievements
The UltraBright Immersive Camp programs aim to arm students not just with knowledge of mathematical algorithms and programming syntax, but the confidence and mentality of a seasoned engineer to tackle unknown problems using state-of-the-art technologies.

By challenging students to complete end-to-end machine learning and AI applications, students learn to see learning as a means to an end, and not as an end in itself. The AI capstone projects are developed by machine learning engineers who have years of industry experience building and productionizing machine learning and AI products, the key in delivering this learning insight is to build practical applications where students can easily see the connection to real-world problem solving, instead of working on trivial toy problems. These AI capstone projects include fully automated self-driving robots, conversational chatbots that perform better than Siri and multiple data science projects from Kaggle competitions submitted by leading companies. Students who complete the camp gain a working knowledge of these state-of-the-art technologies, and are more than capable of expanding the work in their own ways.
